Sisig

Ingredients:

  • 1/4 cup of pork belly

  • 2-3 red chillies

  • 1 medium onion

  • 3 cloves of garlic

  • 1 tablespoon calamansi

  • 1/4 cup of mayonnaise

  • 1 egg

  • 1/4 cup of soy sauce

Procedure:

  1. Heat vegetable oil in a large skillet or frying pan over medium heat. Add the finely chopped pig's head (and optional pork belly) and fry until crispy and golden brown, stirring occasionally to avoid burning.

  2. Stir to combine with the onions and garlic. Pour in the soy sauce, vinegar, and calamansi or lemon juice. Add sugar (optional) and ground black pepper. Stir everything together, allowing the vinegar to cook off and the flavors to meld. Taste the mixture and adjust seasoning with salt, pepper, or more soy sauce if needed.

  3. For a creamy version, you can stir in mayonnaise at this stage and mix well. If you're using an egg, push the sisig mixture to the side of the pan, crack the egg in the empty space, and scramble it. Once cooked, mix it into the rest of the sisig.

  4. For an authentic sisig experience, serve the dish on a sizzling plate (you can heat the plate beforehand). If you prefer, you can also serve it on a regular plate.
