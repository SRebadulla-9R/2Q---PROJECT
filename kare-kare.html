Kare-kare

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb of oxtail

  • 1/2 lb of beef tripe

  • 1 medium onion

  • 1-2 tomatoes

  • 1 teaspoon of fish sauce

  • Salt & pepper

  • 6-8 cups of chicken broth

  • 1 bunch of pechay

  • 1/2 lb of string beans

  • 1 cup of peanut butter

  • 1 banana heart

  • 1/2 of kalabasa

Procedure:

  1. Prepare the meat: In a large pot, add the oxtail and beef tripe (if using). Cover with water and bring to a boil. Skim off any scum or impurities that rise to the top.

  2. Simmer the meat: Once the water is clear, add the onion, tomatoes, fish sauce, and some salt and pepper. Reduce the heat and simmer for 1.5 to 2 hours, or until the meat is tender and the oxtail is almost falling off the bone. If using beef tripe, ensure it is soft and tender as well.

  3. Cook the vegetables: While the meat is simmering, prepare the vegetables. In a separate pot, blanch the banana heart (if using), pechay, eggplant, string beans, and squash by boiling them until they are tender but still vibrant in color. Set aside.

  4. Make the peanut sauce: In a separate pan, heat some annatto oil (or regular oil) over medium heat. Add the peanut butter and stir until it begins to melt and become smooth. Add the ground rice (this will help thicken the sauce) and soy sauce. Gradually pour in some of the beef broth, stirring constantly to create a smooth, thick sauce. If the sauce is too thick, add more broth or water to achieve the desired consistency.

  5. Combine the peanut sauce and meat: Once the meat is tender, strain out the broth to remove the onion and tomatoes, and then add the peanut sauce mixture into the meat pot. Stir well to combine and let it simmer for another 10-15 minutes to meld the flavors together.

  6. Serve: Transfer the kare-kare to a serving dish. Serve with a side of bagoong (fermented shrimp paste) for a savory and salty contrast, along with steamed rice.
