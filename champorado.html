Champorado

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup of glutionous rice

  • Cocoa powder

  • 1/2 cup of sugar

  • 4 cups of water

  • condensed milk

  • evaporated milk

  • 1/4 teaspoon of salt

Procedure:

  1. Rinse the rice: Wash the glutinous rice thoroughly under cold running water until the water runs clear.

  2. In a medium pot, bring 4 cups of water to a boil. Add the glutinous rice and cook over medium heat, stirring occasionally, until the rice becomes soft and sticky (about 20-25 minutes). If needed, add more water during cooking to prevent the rice from sticking to the bottom of the pot.

  3. While the rice is cooking, in a small bowl, mix the cocoa powder with a little bit of warm water to make a smooth chocolate paste. Set aside.

  4. Pour in the coconut milk and coconut cream, and stir to combine. Add a pinch of salt and brown sugar to balance the flavors. Bring to a gentle simmer and cook for 10-12 minutes until the sauce thickens and the flavors meld.

  5. Combine cocoa with rice: Once the rice is cooked and soft, add the chocolate paste to the pot of rice. Stir well to combine. Add the sugar and salt (if using), then continue to cook for another 5-10 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the mixture thickens to your desired consistency. If it gets too thick, you can add more water or milk to loosen it up.

  6. Ladle the champorado into bowls. You can serve it hot or cold, and for extra creaminess, drizzle with evaporated milk or condensed milk on top.
