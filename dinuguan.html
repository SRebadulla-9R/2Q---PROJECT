Dinuguan

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb of pork belly

  • 1 cup of pig's blood

  • 1 medium onion

  • 3-4 cloves of garlic

  • 2-3 long green chili peppers

  • 1-2 tablespoons of fish sauce

  • 1/2 teaspoon of black pepper & salt

  • 2 tablespoons of fish sauce

  • 2-3 tablespoons of vinegar/p>

  • 1-2 dried bay leaves

  • 1 cup of coconut milk

Procedure:

  1. Cook the pork: In a large pan, heat the oil over medium heat. Add the pork belly pieces and cook until they are lightly browned and some fat has rendered out./p>

  2. Sauté aromatics: Add the minced garlic and chopped onion to the pan. Cook, stirring occasionally, until the onions become translucent.

  3. Add the vinegar: Pour in the vinegar and let it simmer for 2-3 minutes to cook off the strong vinegar taste.

  4. Add the blood and liquids: Pour in the pig’s blood, stirring it in to prevent curdling. Add the water or pork broth and bring to a simmer.

  5. Add coconut milk (optional): For a creamier version, add coconut milk. Stir and let it simmer for another 5 minutes.

  6. Serve: Once the dish has reached your desired consistency, remove from heat and serve hot with steamed rice or puto.
